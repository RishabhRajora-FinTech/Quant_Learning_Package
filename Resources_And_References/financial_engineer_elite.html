Probability: From Intuition to Mastery MONTHS 1-4 ▾
1Foundations (Weeks 1-4)
Counting: Permutations, combinations, multinomial coefficients, stars-and-bars, inclusion-exclusion, double counting
Conditional probability: Chain rule, total probability, Bayes theorem — until it's reflexive, not calculated
Random variables: PMF, PDF, CDF, joint/marginal distributions, independence vs uncorrelatedness (know the difference!)
ALL distributions: Bernoulli, Binomial, Geometric, Neg Binomial, Hypergeometric, Poisson, Uniform, Exponential, Normal, Gamma, Beta, Chi-sq, t, F — for each know: mean, variance, MGF, when it arises, relationship to others
2Intermediate (Weeks 5-10)
Expectation mastery: Linearity (even without independence), iterated expectations E[E[X|Y]]=E[X], indicator trick, LOTUS
Variance: Var(aX+bY), conditional variance formula Var(X)=E[Var(X|Y)]+Var(E[X|Y]), covariance and correlation
Generating functions: MGF to derive sums. PGF for discrete. Characteristic functions (Fourier) for heavy tails
Convergence: In distribution, in probability, almost surely, in Lp — know which implies which
Limit theorems: LLN (weak+strong), CLT (Lindeberg condition), Berry-Esseen for convergence rate
Markov chains: Chapman-Kolmogorov, state classification, stationary distributions, ergodic theorem, hitting times
3Advanced (Weeks 11-16)
Martingales: Definition, optional stopping, convergence, Doob's maximal inequality. Connection to fair games and pricing.
Poisson processes: Merging, splitting, thinning. Connection to exponential. Compound Poisson for jump models.
Order statistics: Distribution of max/min, joint density. Useful for barrier pricing intuition.
Extreme value theory: GEV (Gumbel, Frechet, Weibull). Block maxima vs peaks-over-threshold.
Concentration inequalities: Markov, Chebyshev, Hoeffding, Chernoff, Azuma. Critical for estimation.

Mastery test: Can you solve 90% of Xinfeng Zhou's problems in under 5 min? If yes, Tier 2. If you create novel variations and solve those, Tier 3.

Stochastic Calculus: The Heart of Quant Finance MONTHS 3-8 ▾
1Brownian Motion (Weeks 1-3)
Construction: Random walk limit, Donsker's invariance principle
Properties: Independent increments, Gaussian, continuous paths, nowhere differentiable, unbounded variation, quadratic variation = t
Key results: Reflection principle, maximum distribution, hitting times, Levy's characterization
2Itô Calculus (Weeks 4-8)
Itô integral: Why Riemann fails (BM has infinite variation). Ito's choice: evaluate at left endpoint. Zero mean, isometry, martingale.
Itô's lemma: df = (∂f/∂t + μ·∂f/∂x + ½σ²·∂²f/∂x²)dt + σ·∂f/∂x·dW — the extra ½σ² is everything
GBM: Apply Itô's to log(S) → log-normal returns → foundation of Black-Scholes
SDEs: Existence/uniqueness (Lipschitz), strong vs weak solutions, Ornstein-Uhlenbeck
Multi-dimensional: Correlated BMs, matrix formulation for basket/multi-asset models
3Measure Change & Pricing (Weeks 9-14)
Radon-Nikodym: How to change probability measures. The density process.
Girsanov: Under Q, drift changes but vol stays. dWt_Q = dWt_P + θ·dt
Risk-neutral pricing: E^Q[e^(-rT) · payoff]. WHY: replicating portfolio + no-arbitrage = unique price
Fundamental theorems: No arbitrage = EMM exists. Complete market = unique EMM.
Feynman-Kac: PDE solution = conditional expectation. Bridges BS PDE and risk-neutral pricing.
4Beyond Black-Scholes (Weeks 15-20)
Local vol: Dupire's formula. Calibration to vanillas. Forward smile dynamics weakness.
Stochastic vol: Heston (CIR for variance, Fourier inversion). SABR for rates/FX. Bergomi.
Jump diffusion: Merton (Poisson+GBM). Kou double-exponential. Bates = Heston+jumps.
Rough volatility: Fractional BM, rough Heston. Cutting-edge — signals genuine depth.
Free boundary: American options as optimal stopping. Variational inequalities.
Linear Algebra for Quant Applications MONTHS 2-4 ▾
  • PCA for risk: Eigendecomposition of correlation matrix → principal risk factors. Reduce 500 rate scenarios to 3 factors.
  • Cholesky: Generate correlated MC paths. C = LL', Z_corr = L · Z_indep.
  • Positive definiteness: WHY correlation matrices must be PD. How to fix broken ones (nearest PD, eigenvalue clipping).
  • SVD: Dimensionality reduction, pseudo-inverse, low-rank approximation for calibration.
  • Matrix calculus: Gradient of portfolio variance w.r.t. weights. Mean-variance optimization.
  • Sparse matrices: Large FD grids. Tri-diagonal solvers for implicit schemes.
Numerical Methods: Where Math Meets Code MONTHS 4-8 ▾
1Monte Carlo Methods
Basic MC: LLN convergence, CLT error rate (1/√N). Always compute standard error.
Variance reduction: Antithetic variates, control variates, importance sampling, stratified sampling
Path generation: Euler-Maruyama, Milstein (higher order). QMC: Sobol/Halton (O(1/N) vs O(1/√N))
Least-squares MC: Longstaff-Schwartz for American/Bermudan. Regression basis functions.
Greeks by MC: Bump-reprice, pathwise (IPA), likelihood ratio. When each works.
2Finite Differences (PDE Solvers)
Explicit (simple, conditionally stable CFL), Implicit, Crank-Nicolson (industry standard, unconditionally stable)
Boundary conditions: Dirichlet, Neumann, far-field asymptotic. ADI for multi-dimensional PDEs.
Coordinate transforms: Log-price grid, non-uniform grids for barriers.
3Calibration & Optimization
Root finding: Newton-Raphson (implied vol), bisection, Brent's method
Least squares: Levenberg-Marquardt for model calibration. Regularization.
Global optimization: Differential evolution, simulated annealing for non-convex (Heston, SABR)
Convex: QP for portfolio optimization. SOCP for CVaR minimization.
Statistics & Machine Learning for Quant MONTHS 6-10 ▾
  • Time series: AR, MA, ARMA, ARIMA, GARCH, regime-switching, cointegration
  • Bayesian methods: Prior/posterior, MCMC, conjugate priors for calibration
  • Regression: OLS, Ridge, LASSO, elastic net. Cross-validation. Factor models.
  • Tree models: Random forests, XGBoost/LightGBM for credit scoring, failure prediction
  • Neural networks: LSTMs for time series, autoencoders for anomaly detection, deep hedging
  • RL: Optimal execution (Almgren-Chriss), market making as RL. Cutting edge.
  • Causal inference: A/B testing for strategies, avoiding spurious correlations

Derivatives Pricing: The Complete Framework CORE MASTERY ▾
1Vanilla Options (Know Absolutely Cold)
Black-Scholes: 3 derivations. All assumptions. Which break in practice.
Put-Call Parity: Derive from no-arbitrage. Spot mispricing. Extend to dividends, FX.
Greeks: Delta, Gamma (P&L from rehedging), Theta (decay), Vega, Rho, Vanna, Volga
Greek interactions: Gamma-theta tradeoff. Vanna-volga for exotics. Pin risk.
2Volatility: The Most Important Variable
Historical: Close-to-close, Parkinson, Garman-Klass, Yang-Zhang
Implied: Newton-Raphson, Brenner-Subrahmanyam approx, Jaeckel rational (state of art)
Surface: SVI parameterization. Arbitrage-free conditions. Interpolation.
Models: Local vol (Dupire), Stochastic vol (Heston, SABR, Bergomi), Rough vol
VIX: Not ATM IV! Weighted strip of OTM options. Replication formula.
3Exotic Options
Barriers: Up/down, in/out. Reflection principle. Discrete monitoring adjustment.
Asians: Arithmetic (MC) vs geometric (closed-form control variate). Turnbull-Wakeman.
Basket options: Correlation is key. Worst-of, best-of, rainbow.
Autocallables: Conditional early redemption. Huge in structured products.
4Interest Rate Derivatives
Curves: Bootstrapping from deposits, futures, swaps. Log-linear, cubic spline interpolation.
Duration/Convexity: Modified, Macaulay, effective. Key rate durations.
Models: Vasicek, CIR, Hull-White (short rate). HJM framework. LIBOR Market Model.
Products: IRS, swaptions, caps/floors. Multi-curve (OIS discounting post-2008).
Risk Management: Protecting the Firm YOUR EDGE ▾

  • Model risk: Validation framework. Benchmarking. P&L attribution. Sensitivity analysis.
